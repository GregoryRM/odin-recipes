My Journey to Flavour Town

Many years ago, when I was but a young duckling, my dearest Papa sent me to work in the lasanga mines. It was back breaking labour and many nights I would find myself coughing up blood mixed with small lasagna particulates. The noxoius fumes from the mines were so toxic and dangerous that most nights at least one of the many children on my line would sucumb to the gas. Some would collapse, only to be dragged out of the way for another to take their place. Often, those who fell would be left in the mines; their bodies gone by the next day. The unending laberenth held many dangers and of these dangers, the lasagna critters were the most terrifing. Always heard, sometimes smelt, but never seen. They were scavangers at best, often we would attribute the dissapearances of our commrades corpses to the citters. At worst, they would pick off those stupid enough to stray into the darkness or those who doddled during shift change. I can still hear the screams.
